title: "Between Two Worlds"
date: "2026-06-02"
description: "On growing up Australian-Chinese, and why that tension became my greatest asset."
---

I've never fully belonged to one world.

Growing up Australian-Chinese, I was always navigating two sets of expectations, two languages, two ways of seeing. At home, one culture. At school, another. Never quite enough of either.

For a long time, I thought that was a disadvantage.

I was wrong.

That tension — the discomfort of living between worlds — turned out to be the best design training I ever had. You learn to read rooms. To translate. To spot what's lost when two perspectives don't talk to each other.

That's what I do now, just with products instead of people.

I build things that sit at the intersection of East and West, of human intuition and machine intelligence, of craft and commerce. I'm not trying to pick a side. I'm trying to build the bridge.

This journal is where I think out loud about that process.
